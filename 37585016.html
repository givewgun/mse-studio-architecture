Rags to [Ad]Riches : ADR #4: Compressing data to improve label battery performance and save hardware costs

Created by Gun Kaewngarm, last modified on Jun 28, 2024

Context:
In the current system, smart labels transmit uncompressed raw data, which requires significant energy and bandwidth, leading to increased operational costs and reduced battery life. Considering the project's scale—aiming to deploy millions of smart labels—minimizing power consumption and optimizing data transmission becomes crucial for sustainability and cost-efficiency.

Quality Attributes related:

  • Performance (battery)

Decision:

To address these concerns, the decision has been made to implement data compression on the smart labels before transmitting data to the backend. This approach aims to reduce the size of the data packets, thus decreasing transmission time and energy consumption. The system will utilize a lightweight, efficient compression algorithm suitable for the computational limitations of smart labels.

Status:

Agreed

Consequences:

Reduced Power Consumption: By transmitting smaller data packets, labels consume less power, thereby extending the labels' battery life.

Initial Development Cost: Implementing compression requires upfront investment in development and testing to ensure compatibility and effectiveness.
